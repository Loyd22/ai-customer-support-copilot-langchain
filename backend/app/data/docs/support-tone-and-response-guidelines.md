# Support Tone and Response Guidelines

- **Document Title:** Support Tone and Response Guidelines
- **Document Type:** Internal Support Guide
- **Intended Audience:** Support Agents, Team Leads, AI Support Systems
- **Last Updated:** March 12, 2026

## Purpose

Northbeam Goods responses must be clear, calm, accurate, and policy-aligned. The goal is to help the customer understand what is happening, what is possible, and what the next step will be.

## Tone Rules

Responses should be:

- professional
- polite
- direct
- respectful
- easy to understand

Responses should not be:

- defensive
- overly casual
- vague
- argumentative
- overly robotic

## Empathy Rules

Use empathy to acknowledge impact, not to admit fault before review.

Preferred examples:

- “I’m sorry this order did not arrive as expected.”
- “I understand why this is frustrating.”
- “Thanks for sending those details.”

Avoid exaggerated or empty phrases such as:

- “I completely understand exactly how you feel.”
- “No worries” in serious or high-value complaints
- “This should definitely be fixed today” unless confirmed

## Policy Alignment Rules

Agents and AI must:

- explain the relevant policy clearly
- apply the same timeline and eligibility rules every time
- avoid offering unofficial exceptions without approval
- state when review is still in progress

Do not say:

- “We always refund cases like this”
- “Your package is definitely lost”
- “Your cancellation is guaranteed”
- “Your manager request will result in an exception”

## Promise Control

Never promise:

- a refund before eligibility is confirmed
- a replacement before proof is reviewed
- a cancellation after the order has reached **Packed** or later
- a delivery date the carrier has not confirmed
- a specific bank posting date for refunds

Use safer wording:

- “Once the return is received and approved, the refund is typically initiated within 3 business days.”
- “If the order has not moved to Packed or Shipped, cancellation may still be possible.”
- “We’re reviewing the tracking history before we confirm next steps.”

## Structure for Good Responses

A strong support response should usually include:

1. acknowledgment of the issue
2. clear statement of what was checked
3. policy-based next step
4. request for missing details, if needed
5. realistic timeframe

## AI-Specific Response Rules

AI should:

- answer within documented policy
- ask for missing order information when needed
- flag cases that meet escalation rules
- avoid improvising operational promises
- use customer-friendly language without sounding scripted

AI should not:

- invent order details
- assume refund approval
- overstate certainty
- give legal, fraud, or billing decisions

## When to Escalate in the Response

If a case needs escalation, say so clearly without overpromising.

Preferred example:

- “I’m sending this case to our specialist team for review. They’ll check the details and follow up with the next steps.”

Avoid:

- “A manager will fix this for you right away.”
- “You will definitely receive a full refund after escalation.”

## What to Do When Policy Does Not Favor the Customer

When the answer is no:

- be clear
- be respectful
- explain why
- offer the next valid option

Example:

- “Your order has already shipped, so cancellation is no longer available. Once it is delivered, you may request a return if the item is eligible under our return policy.”

## Consistency Between Agents and AI

If AI and human support both answer the same question, the core answer must match on:

- return window
- refund timing
- shipping timelines
- cancellation cutoff
- lost package threshold
- escalation conditions

## Consistency Summary

- **Fictional Company Name:** Northbeam Goods
- **Business Type:** Online retail company selling physical home, office, and lifestyle products
- **Order Processing Time:** 1 to 2 business days after successful payment
- **Standard Shipping Time:** 3 to 5 business days after dispatch
- **Express Shipping Time:** 1 to 2 business days after dispatch
- **Refund Request Window:** 30 calendar days from delivery for eligible returns
- **Damage / Defect / Wrong Item Reporting Window:** 7 calendar days from delivery
- **Return Inspection Timeline:** within 3 business days of receipt
- **Refund Initiation Timeline:** within 3 business days after approved return inspection or approved cancellation
- **Refund Posting Timeline:** usually 5 to 10 business days depending on payment provider
- **Cancellation Cutoff:** cancellation generally allowed only before order reaches Packed status
- **Lost Package Threshold:** no tracking movement for 7 calendar days after the latest estimated delivery date, or carrier confirms loss
- **Company-Paid Return Shipping:** damaged, defective, incorrect, or fulfillment-error cases
- **Customer-Paid Return Shipping:** change-of-mind or customer-error returns
- **Partial Refund Triggers:** missing parts, unexpected use, condition mismatch, approved exception handling
- **Escalation Triggers:** manager request after policy explanation, fraud suspicion, legal threat, chargeback risk, high-value order over $500, repeated unresolved complaint, conflicting lost-package evidence, serious safety concern
