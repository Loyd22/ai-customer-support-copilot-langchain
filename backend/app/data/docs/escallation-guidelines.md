# Escalation Guidelines

- **Document Title:** Escalation Guidelines
- **Document Type:** Internal Support Guide
- **Intended Audience:** Support Agents, Team Leads, AI Support Systems
- **Last Updated:** March 12, 2026

## Purpose

These guidelines define when a Northbeam Goods case must be escalated beyond standard frontline handling. Escalation is required when the issue carries higher financial, legal, reputational, fraud, or customer-risk impact.

## What Counts as Escalation

Escalation is required when any of the following apply:

- customer requests a manager after policy has been explained clearly
- suspected fraud, account takeover, or payment abuse
- chargeback threat or active billing dispute
- order value exceeds **$500** and the issue is unresolved
- repeat complaint on the same unresolved problem after two prior contacts
- lost package with conflicting delivery evidence
- safety concern involving product injury, smoke, overheating, breakage risk, or child hazard
- legal threat, regulator complaint, or formal written demand
- abusive or high-risk behavior that may require account review
- media, influencer, or public social complaint with credible reputational risk
- policy exception request outside standard agent authority

## Priority Levels

### P1 Critical
Use for:

- safety incidents
- legal threats
- fraud or account compromise
- active payment dispute or chargeback
- severe public escalation risk

**Action:** Escalate immediately. Create internal alert and assign to lead or specialist within **15 minutes**, no later than **30 minutes**.

### P2 High
Use for:

- lost package with conflicting evidence
- high-value order issues over **$500**
- repeated unresolved complaint
- customer demanding manager involvement with credible service failure
- replacement or refund dispute involving multiple prior contacts

**Action:** Escalate within **1 business hour**.

### P3 Standard
Use for:

- normal returns, refunds, shipping delays, and replacement requests that fit policy
- routine dissatisfaction without risk markers

**Action:** Frontline agent handles unless new escalation trigger appears.

### P4 Low
Use for:

- general questions
- policy clarification
- pre-purchase support
- tracking questions with normal movement

**Action:** Standard handling.

## Fraud, Legal, and Billing Cases

Frontline agents and AI systems must not make final decisions on:

- fraud accusations
- chargeback reversals
- legal interpretations
- threats involving regulators, attorneys, or formal claims
- requests to bypass identity or payment verification

These cases must be routed to the appropriate specialist or support lead.

## Repeated Complaints

Escalate when:

- the same customer contacts support **three times** about the same unresolved issue
- the customer was promised a follow-up and no action occurred
- prior notes show conflicting answers from support
- the customer has already received a policy explanation but the case remains unresolved due to operational failure

## Manager Requests

A customer request for a manager should be escalated when:

- policy explanation alone did not resolve the issue
- the customer disputes fairness of an exception decision
- the case includes repeated service failure or unusual impact

A customer request for a manager does not automatically mean policy changes will be granted.

## Expected Response Behavior

When escalating:

- summarize the issue clearly
- state what has already been checked
- include order number, timeline, and customer ask
- avoid emotional language in internal notes
- do not promise approval before review
- tell the customer the case is being reviewed by the appropriate team

## AI-Specific Rule

AI may identify escalation triggers, but it must not:

- approve policy exceptions outside documented rules
- make legal or fraud judgments
- guarantee refunds, replacements, or cancellations before system confirmation
