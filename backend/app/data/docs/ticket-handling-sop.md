# Ticket Handling SOP

- **Document Title:** Ticket Handling SOP
- **Document Type:** Internal Support Guide
- **Intended Audience:** Support Agents, Team Leads, AI Support Systems
- **Last Updated:** March 12, 2026

## Purpose

This SOP defines how support tickets must be created, updated, routed, and closed at Northbeam Goods.

## Ticket Statuses

Use the following statuses consistently:

- **New**: ticket created but not yet reviewed
- **Open**: agent is actively working the case
- **Pending Customer**: waiting for customer reply or proof
- **Pending Internal**: waiting on warehouse, carrier, billing, or lead review
- **Escalated**: assigned to senior support, lead, or specialist team
- **Resolved**: solution provided, awaiting final confirmation if needed
- **Closed**: no further action required

## First Review Workflow

### Step 1: Verify the Request
Confirm:

- order number or account identifier
- customer issue type
- current order status
- whether proof is needed

### Step 2: Classify the Ticket
Tag the ticket as one primary type:

- shipping
- refund
- return
- replacement
- cancellation
- damaged item
- wrong item
- lost package
- billing
- account issue
- escalation request

### Step 3: Check Policy and Case Data
Review:

- policy eligibility
- order timeline
- prior contacts
- tracking details
- notes from other teams
- refund or replacement history

### Step 4: Take Action
Do one of the following:

- answer directly
- request missing information
- send approved next steps
- route to internal team
- escalate

## Response Expectations

### Standard Cases
- first response within **4 business hours**

### High Priority Cases
- first response within **1 business hour**

### Critical Cases
- internal escalation within **15 minutes**
- customer acknowledgment as soon as possible

## Note-Taking Rules

Every ticket note must include:

- what the customer reported
- what was verified
- what action was taken
- what the customer was told
- next step and owner

Notes must be:

- factual
- concise
- chronological
- free of blame or sarcasm

Bad note example:  
“Customer is angry again and keeps complaining.”

Good note example:  
“Customer reported missing package despite delivered scan. Advised 48-hour wait period and requested building mailroom check. Follow-up set for next business day.”

## Proof Collection Rules

Request proof only when necessary. Common examples:

- damage photos
- package photos
- shipping label photo
- defect video where needed

Do not ask for duplicate proof if it is already sufficient in the ticket.

## Handoff Rules

Move a ticket to **Pending Internal** when waiting on:

- warehouse confirmation
- carrier investigation
- billing verification
- lead approval

Move a ticket to **Escalated** when it meets the Escalation Guidelines.

## Resolution Rules

A ticket may be marked **Resolved** when:

- the approved action has been completed
- the customer has received clear next steps
- no further internal action is pending

A ticket may be marked **Closed** when:

- the issue is fully completed, or
- the customer has not replied after a reasonable follow-up cycle and no action remains

## Reopen Rules

Reopen the ticket when:

- the customer replies with new information
- the prior action failed
- the same issue was marked resolved in error
